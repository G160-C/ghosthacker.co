1. Introduction to Ethical Hacking

Ethical hacking involves legally breaking into computers and devices to test an organization's defenses. Unlike malicious hackers, ethical hackers have permission to test systems and help improve security.

2. Setting Up Your Lab

Recommended tools:

  • Kali Linux (VM)
  • VirtualBox or VMware
  • Metasploitable 2 / DVWA for practice

Always test in isolated environments to avoid legal issues.

3. Information Gathering

Also called reconnaissance, this step involves collecting data about your target using tools like:

  • WHOIS
  • nslookup
  • Shodan
  • Google Dorking

4. Scanning & Enumeration

Use scanning tools to find open ports and services: 

nmap -sS -A target_ip

Enumeration digs deeper: getting usernames, shares, services from open ports.

5. Exploitation Basics

6. Reporting & Ethics

Always document your process. A good pentest report includes:

  • Findings with severity
  • Steps to reproduce
  • Mitigation suggestions
  • Screenshots/logs

Ethics matter. Never test without permission. Follow your local laws.
